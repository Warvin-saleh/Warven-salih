ABOUT

Learn more about me, my background, and what motivates me.
2017

Early Passion for Art My name is Warvin Saleh. I am born on May 12, 1999. Since childhood, I have had a passion for good deeds and unique dreams. As a child, I was intelligent and industrious. I always took part in exhibitions every year, showcasing my work.

2019

I continued pursuing my artistic career until I joined the art class of teacher Kly Nemat in 2019. During this period, I improved significantly, understanding myself better. Some of my paintings from that class, along with my certificate, gave me more motivation. I advanced my skills and participated in many exhibitions. My first significant painting was titled "Ezzat Zaiti," and it was presented on April 30, 2019, by the artist Azhar Hazem Beg.

2020

I immersed myself more deeply in the art field, realizing my growth over time and aiming to become a prominent Kurdish artist. On December 10, 2020, I participated in the memorial exhibition for the martyr artist Salman with a painting titled "The Brave Girl of Hope" at the Cultural Center of the Kurdistan Artists Syndicate in Duhok/Zakho, under the guidance of the Directorate of Art and Culture.

2021

On September 16, 2021, I took part in the "Palette Exhibition" with a group of talented artists from Duhok and Zakho at the Duhok Gallery, showcasing several of my paintings along with my certificate. In 2021, I became a student at the University of Social Sciences in the Art Department, hoping to progress and fulfill my academic and artistic potential. As an art student, I was informed about participating in an activity at the private Zakho Institute, where the Institute's Art and Culture Department awarded me an honorary certificate and praised my work. At the university, I had many opportunities to advance and improve my skills. As a female artist, I aimed to create a unique style and innovate in my work.

2022

On September 18, 2022, as an artist, I participated in an event held in collaboration with the "Nojen Group" and under the supervision of the Directorate of Art and Culture, with 80 other artists. Throughout the year, I got to know many young artists, and we participated in various exhibitions. This led to forming a group called "The Hope Group" with the cooperation of my friend Erin Rabin and art professor Alaa. The group's achievements resulted in special exhibitions, and the Zakho Women's Association appreciated our work.

Hobbies

My goal is to convey my message through art and never lose the emotions and sensations I experience through painting. I hope that all these emotions will be transformed into valuable creations that represent my journey, my story, and the essence of my paintings.
